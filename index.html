Hi. I'm Isaac Malsky.

I'm a NASA Postdoctoral Program (NPP) Fellow at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory, where I study the atmospheres of exoplanets. I build three-dimensional general circulation models (GCMs) of hot Jupiters and sub-Neptunes, model how clouds, hazes, and magnetic effects shape what we observe, and develop machine-learning tools to accelerate radiative transfer.

I completed my PhD in Astronomy at the University of Michigan in 2024, advised by Emily Rauscher, following a B.A. in Physics from the University of Chicago. Feel free to reach out.

See what I work on

Research

I work on the physics of exoplanet atmospheres — connecting three-dimensional models to the spectra and phase curves we actually observe.

3D Atmospheric Circulation

General circulation models of hot Jupiters and sub-Neptunes that link three-dimensional temperature, wind, and chemical structure to observable emission and transmission signatures.

Clouds, Hazes & Radiative Transfer

How radiatively active clouds, photochemical hazes, and the choice of radiative-transfer scheme reshape spectra — and how magnetic effects influence ultrahot Jupiters.

Atmospheric Escape & Evolution

Photoevaporative mass loss and the coupled thermal and compositional evolution of planetary envelopes across the radius valley.

Machine Learning for Atmospheres

Transformer-based emulators that accelerate radiative transfer for planetary atmospheres by orders of magnitude, making large model grids tractable.
